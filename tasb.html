TASB Jan 2018 – Dec 2019 · Senior Digital Project Manager

Building an agency
from the inside.

Running a fee-for-service internal digital agency serving Texas school boards — strategy, delivery, analytics, and the governance model that made it work.

Program Management Analytics Web Delivery Process Design Requirements Stakeholder Management
01
The situation

An internal agency model serving Texas school boards.

The Texas Association of School Boards (TASB) operated an internal digital team that functioned as a fee-for-service agency — serving multiple internal clients and member organizations across a portfolio of web and digital projects. I was hired to bring structure to the delivery model, define how strategy got translated into executable plans, and build the measurement foundation that would let the team demonstrate value.

The environment was member-driven: school boards and district leaders were the clients, and their needs ranged across public web experiences, secure member portals, content strategy, and digital marketing. The challenge was doing rigorous digital strategy and delivery inside an organization whose core mission was not digital.

02
My role

Senior Digital Project Manager — strategy, delivery, and governance.

I led strategy and delivery for the internal agency, owning cost estimates, statements of work, budgets, invoicing, and the delivery of public and secure member web experiences. I served as a client-facing advisor to internal stakeholders, guiding digital strategy, scope decisions, and prioritization across concurrent initiatives.

I also built the delivery and governance processes that the team needed to operate consistently across multiple concurrent clients — intake, estimation, project documentation, and the measurement model that connected member behavior to business goals.

03
Discovery

40+ hours of stakeholder focus groups.

Before writing a single requirement, I ran an extended stakeholder discovery process. The goal was to translate the needs of multiple internal client groups — each with different priorities, vocabularies, and definitions of success — into a prioritized backlog of measurable objectives and site capabilities.

The focus groups were structured to surface the business goals underneath the feature requests. What do members actually need from this experience? What behavior would tell us the site is working? What are the things that look important but aren't, and what are the things nobody has thought to ask for yet?

04
Analytics model

Connecting member behavior to business goals.

One of the most significant pieces of work at TASB was building an analytics model that connected Google Analytics signals to Salesforce member data. The goal was to visualize the user journey in a way that showed not just what members were doing on the site, but what it meant for the organization's goals.

I built the data visualizations in Tableau and Power BI — models that let non-technical stakeholders see the relationship between web behavior and membership outcomes. This was the infrastructure that let the digital team make the case for its own value in a concrete, data-driven way.

Data integration

Tableau and Power BI dashboards

05
Results

A functioning agency model with measurement built in.

Ran 40+ hours of internal stakeholder focus groups that produced a prioritized backlog tied to measurable business objectives — not a wish list.

Built delivery and governance processes — intake, estimation, SOWs, project documentation — that improved accountability and consistency across a multi-client portfolio.

Connected Google Analytics and Salesforce member data into Tableau and Power BI dashboards that gave stakeholders a concrete view of web performance against business goals.

Delivered public and secure member web experiences for Texas school districts — navigating the regulatory, political, and member-relations complexity of a statewide member organization.

Left behind process templates, governance documentation, and an analytics infrastructure that outlasted the engagement.
