Regional Healthcare System Nov 2014 – Jan 2018 · Senior Digital Strategist

Enterprise at
a human scale.

Leading digital strategy across a regulated healthcare system — portals, scheduling, migrations, and stakeholders who did not always agree.

Enterprise PM CMS Web Delivery Sitecore Healthcare Requirements Stakeholder Management
01
The situation

A large healthcare system with a lot of digital to manage.

Ascension — Seton Healthcare Family was one of the largest faith-based healthcare systems in central Texas. The digital team operated as an internal agency, serving clinical, operational, and marketing stakeholders across multiple facilities and service lines. The environment was regulated, highly stakeholder-driven, and running on legacy infrastructure in several areas.

The work ranged from patient-facing website experiences to a secure physician portal, online scheduling integration, a major Sitecore CMS migration, and the digital marketing infrastructure that supported it all. None of these programs were simple and most involved coordination across IT, security, compliance, legal, clinical operations, and external vendors simultaneously.

02
My role

Senior Digital Strategist — client-facing delivery lead.

I served as the primary delivery partner and advisor to clinical, operational, and marketing stakeholders. This meant owning requirements discovery, guiding scope and feasibility decisions, coordinating across IT, security, compliance, vendors, and internal teams, and ensuring that what we delivered met both business goals and regulatory requirements.

The internal agency model meant I was running multiple concurrent programs, each with its own stakeholder group, timeline, and risk profile. I also directed CRM-driven digital marketing and engagement programs alongside the platform delivery work.

03
The programs

Four major initiatives across four years.

Sitecore CMS migration

Led requirements and delivery planning for the migration of a large multisite Seton web presence into Sitecore. Coordinated content strategy, technology planning, redirect governance, and stakeholder alignment across a complex, multi-year effort. My boss designed the architecture; I owned client delivery within it.

Secure physician portal

Led requirements discovery and delivery for a secure portal serving physicians and clinical staff. Navigated single sign-on complexity, Active Directory integration, multiple clinical systems, and strict security and compliance requirements. Documented provider types, access levels, and integration dependencies.

Online scheduling

Led the patient-facing online scheduling experience — requirements, vendor coordination, and stakeholder alignment across clinical operations and marketing. Balanced patient UX goals with the constraints of legacy scheduling systems underneath.

Digital marketing and analytics

Directed CRM-driven engagement programs and digital marketing operations. Used analytics to evaluate performance and guide decisions. Managed campaign briefs, vendor relationships, and multi-channel execution across patient and provider audiences.

04
How I worked

Requirements depth and stakeholder calm.

Healthcare delivery environments have real stakes — patient data, clinical workflows, regulatory compliance, and stakeholders who are not always aligned on priorities. My job was to hold all of that without letting the complexity paralyze the work.

I built detailed requirements documentation, created process maps and project briefs that let different stakeholders see their piece of the program clearly, and ran the kind of discovery sessions that surface the real constraints before they become launch-day problems.

I also maintained the relationship between the technical team and the clinical stakeholders — two groups with genuinely different vocabularies and risk tolerances — and made sure what we built actually reflected both.

05
Results

Four years of enterprise digital delivery in a regulated environment.

Led delivery of a secure physician portal serving multiple provider types across a complex authentication and access control environment.

Contributed to Sitecore migration planning and client delivery — requirements documentation, content strategy, redirect governance, and stakeholder coordination across a multi-year program.

Developed a project brief template and intake process that became the standard for how the digital team scoped and kicked off new programs.

Built and maintained working relationships with clinical, IT, marketing, compliance, and legal stakeholders simultaneously — the kind of cross-functional trust that makes complex regulated-environment delivery possible.

Rich artifact library: sitemaps, wireframes, requirements documents, scope documents, project timelines, and process documentation — some of the most detailed work product in this portfolio.
