Flip Cup · Round Canvas

Spacey, not oceanic.

Same paint. Same session. The round canvas changed what it became.

Photo coming

Flip cup on round canvas · Arteza metallics, Azteca Ultramarine Blue · residual silicone from Bioluminescent session

The origin

Same session. Different conversation.

A flip cup uses what’s left. After the Bioluminescent pour was finished, the cups still had paint — ultramarine blue and blue metallics, layered in the order they’d been used. Most of the silicone oil had already floated to the surface during the first pour. A trace amount remained. Enough.

I stacked the colors into a single cup, inverted it onto a round canvas, and lifted. The paint underneath spread on its own. Then tilt work to move it across the surface, torch pass to release the bubbles. Done.

The format

No horizon.

The format changes everything. A rectangular canvas has orientation — top, bottom, implied horizon. A round canvas has none of that. There’s no up. The composition radiates from wherever the flip cup landed, and the tilt spreads it without a preferred direction.

That’s part of why this piece reads differently than Bioluminescent. Same palette, same session, same chemistry. But the round format removed the horizon line, and without a horizon, the result stopped reading as ocean and started reading as space.

The distinction

Spacey, not oceanic.

That distinction matters. The Bioluminescent piece has depth — you read downward into it, the way you’d read into water. This one has distance. You read outward from the center, the way you’d read a star field or a nebula. The cells are the same cellular chemistry, but in a radial field without a horizon they become something else — less biological, more astronomical.

I find it weirdly soothing. The blue is the same blue. The mood is completely different.

What connects them

The field the creature lives in.

This piece lives in direct conversation with Bioluminescent. It uses the same paint, from the same session, with the same silicone chemistry still faintly active. If Bioluminescent is the creature, this is the field it lives in.

They’re not a series. But they belong together.
