Flip Cup Pour · Functional Substrate

When the bracket is flat, it’s a painting. When it’s open, it’s a shelf.

One object. Two states. The cat has claimed it either way.

Photo coming

Flip cup pour on wood board · green, teal, blue · fold-down wall brackets, bolted into studs

The problem it solved

A clean desk.

The desk was cluttered. Speaker, pen cup, hand lotion, whatever else needs to be within arm’s reach — all of it competing for surface space. A side table would have been in the way. A wall shelf would have been permanent and static.

A fold-down shelf is neither. When it’s open it holds everything. When it folds flat against the wall, the clutter disappears and the painting comes back. The desk stays clear.

Built to stay up

Bolted into studs.

The brackets are bolted into studs. This is not a decorative shelf that holds a candle and a small plant. It holds a speaker, a full pen cup, and whatever else lands on it on a given day. Occasionally it holds a cat — close enough to the desk to feel involved, far enough away from the keyboard to not be a problem. It is structurally more than adequate for all of these uses.

Before pouring, I drilled countersunk holes for the mounting screws so the surface would be uninterrupted. The hardware disappears into the pour. The painting reads as a painting, not as a functional object with screws in it.

The pour

Two cups, full coverage.

The shelf is long enough that one cup wouldn’t cover it, so I used two flip cups, positioned to meet in the middle and cover the full length. Green, teal, and blue over the full surface. Dense cellular development across both zones. The silicone chemistry opened the cells evenly across most of the board.

There’s a spot near one edge where too much paint pooled during the drain. The cells there are thicker and the color is heavier than the rest of the surface. I noticed it when it dried. The shelf went up anyway. It’s still there. At the viewing distance a wall-mounted shelf gets, it reads as texture. Up close it’s a record of a decision I made and didn’t make again.
