Lori-vention · Reclaimed Materials

Fully detached. One occupant. Mail delivery available.

Built entirely from reclaimed and found materials. The flag on the mailbox works.

Photo coming

Cardboard A-frame · hand-cut shingles · glass bead texture · felt-lined interior with built-in pillows · scratch-built mailbox

The brief

Zero budget. Non-negotiable quality.

Pax needed a house. The requirements were: structurally sound, appropriately scaled, interior comfortable enough to actually use, and finished to a standard that belonged in a living space rather than a corner. The budget was zero. The materials were whatever was available.

This is what available materials can do when the standard of quality is non-negotiable.

The structure

A-frame on a repurposed box.

The form is an A-frame — two triangular side panels and a peaked roof, open at the back. The foundation is a repurposed cardboard box, painted teal. The lawn surrounding it is packing material painted green, with shaped packing material shrubs running along the sides. Cardboard steps lead up to the front entrance.

The roof is hand-cut cardboard shingles, each one cut individually and layered from the base up the way real shingles are laid, painted in warm brown tones. The whole structure is cardboard. It is rigid, stable, and has been in daily use.

The exterior details

Curb appeal.

Everywhere the deep cobalt blue appears — front panel, sides, steps — the surface is covered in glass beads painted metallic blue and adhered with a glue gun. The dimensional bubble texture is consistent across all the blue surfaces, catching light at every angle. The entrance circle is trimmed in purple.

The mailbox is scratch-built — post, box, and a red flag that raises and lowers. It says Pax. The flag works. There are pipe cleaner flowers at the base, bent and painted pink with yellow centers, planted in a small green ground patch. The landscaping is intentional.

The interior

Upholstered.

The inside of the A-frame was waterproofed first. Plastic bags ironed together into a fused sheet line the interior walls and floor. The heat fuses the layers into a stiff, wipeable surface that protects the cardboard underneath from any cat-related incidents.

Over the plastic liner, felt. The interior is fully felt-lined — walls, floor, and the underside of the roof peak. Pillows are built into the sides and floor: old fabric stuffed and sewn shut, integrated into the felt lining rather than sitting loose inside. The interior is upholstered. Pax uses it regularly, which is the only measure of success that matters for this category of object.
