360training Dec 2021 – Sep 2023 · Web Marketing Manager

Teaching AI to help,
not replace.

Building AI-assisted content workflows that cut drafting time to a fraction — without letting the machine anywhere near the judgment calls.

AI Workflows Web Delivery Ecommerce CMS Analytics Program Management
01
The situation

A high-volume ecommerce environment with a lot of recurring work.

360training is an online compliance training company — a SaaS business selling regulated training courses to B2B and B2C customers across multiple industries. The web properties included Drupal and WebSphere platforms serving high-traffic ecommerce properties, and the content work was high-volume and recurring: category pages, promotional copy, product descriptions, support content, and campaign assets that needed to hit regularly and consistently.

The challenge was scale and speed. The same types of content needed to be produced repeatedly, on deadline, at quality — and the team was doing it manually for everything. There was also significant platform complexity: multiple CMS environments, an LMS underneath, disconnected data systems, and regulatory constraints on certain content types.

02
My role

Web Marketing Manager — platform delivery and operational design.

I owned web delivery for the B2B and B2C ecommerce properties, partnering with SEO, content, design, and engineering teams to improve acquisition, UX, and conversion outcomes. This included program-managing major redesigns and migrations, running UAT across large releases, and building the operational infrastructure that made high-volume delivery sustainable.

I also introduced AI-assisted workflows as a deliberate operational decision — not as an experiment, but as a structured change to how recurring content work got done. This required defining where AI added value, where human judgment was non-negotiable, and building the workflow so that the handoff between the two was clean.

03
Web delivery

Building the delivery infrastructure underneath the content work.

Before the AI workflow work, the more foundational job was building the delivery infrastructure that let the team operate consistently at volume. That meant intake processes, estimation, UAT frameworks, launch checklists, and retrospectives — the machinery that reduces surprises and makes quality repeatable.

Redesigns and migrations

Program-managed major website redesigns and migrations across Drupal and WebSphere environments. Created requirements, workflow documentation, project plans, and acceptance criteria aligned to business goals and regulatory constraints.

UAT and launch readiness

Led UAT and defect triage across large releases. Coordinated fixes across teams and ensured clean handoff to operations. Built reusable UAT templates and launch checklists that reduced chaos at release time.

B2B ecommerce improvements

Separated support requests from leads in the B2B funnel, aligned B2B content to sales goals, and improved UX and conversion performance across category and product pages.

Delivery templates

Built reusable delivery templates and operating rhythms — intake, estimation, retrospectives — that improved quality and reduced surprises during high-volume delivery cycles.

04
AI workflows

AI where it helped. Human judgment where it mattered.

The recurring promotional content problem was real: the same types of copy needed to be produced repeatedly, quickly, at consistent quality. AI was genuinely useful here — it could get to a first draft faster than any human, at any volume, without the fatigue that degrades human copy quality on the fifteenth similar piece.

The workflow I built treated AI as a first-draft accelerator and the human as the quality gate. AI handled: ideation, structure, initial draft, variation generation. Human handled: brand voice calibration, regulatory accuracy, tone judgment, final approval. The result was better first drafts in a fraction of the time, with quality that held up because the judgment layer was never removed.

Promotional copy

AI-assisted drafting for recurring promotional content reduced time-to-first-draft significantly — better results in approximately 10% of the previous time for certain content types.

Navigation and SEO

Used AI to reason through navigation structure and SEO implications, accelerating analysis that previously required multiple rounds of manual research.

Process documentation

Applied AI to accelerate UAT kickoff messaging, retrospective design, executive storytelling, and Jira dashboard configuration — the operational writing that takes time but follows patterns.

The honest framing: I was not using AI because it was trendy. I was using it because the problem — high-volume recurring content, limited human time, consistency pressure — was exactly the type of problem AI is genuinely good at helping with. The constraint I kept was simple: AI touches the draft, humans own the judgment. Every piece that went live had a human who read it and took responsibility for it.

05
Results

Faster delivery, better quality, and a reusable workflow model.

AI-assisted copy workflows reduced time-to-first-draft for recurring promotional content to approximately 10% of previous effort for certain content types, while maintaining brand voice and quality.

Built delivery templates and operating rhythms — intake, estimation, UAT, launch checklists, retrospectives — that improved consistency across a high-volume release environment.

Program-managed major website redesigns and migrations across Drupal and WebSphere, keeping complex cross-team programs on track through requirements, UAT, and launch.

Improved B2B ecommerce performance through funnel separation, content alignment to sales goals, and UX improvements across category and product pages.

Left behind a documented AI workflow model — not just the outputs, but the framework for deciding when AI is appropriate and how to structure the human review layer.
