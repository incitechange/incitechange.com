Craftvention · Found Object Transformation

A side table designed to look like the cat who uses it.

Six requirements. One box. The cat is annoyed about the latch.

Photo coming

Wooden storage box · paint skin lid with resin seal · felt collar band with silver tacks · motion-activated interior light

The brief

Six requirements. One object.

Pax has accessories. Wand toys, crinkle balls, a charging cable for a motorized toy, catnip, miscellaneous items that needed a home near the couch. The brief was: one object that stores all of it, functions as a side table at the correct height, is beautiful enough to live in the living room, and is secure against a cat who knows exactly what’s inside and has opinions about accessing it unsupervised.

The catnip is the issue. He knows it’s in there.

The box

Built to look like him.

The starting point was a wooden storage box. Two latches were added to the front and painted silver — the original box had no mechanism to keep it closed against a determined cat. The latches hold. Pax has tried them.

The exterior needed to look like him. Pax is a ragdoll — dark face and ears, white chest, light body, and a black studded collar he wore for years. All of that is in the box.

The surface

Skin, resin, and a collar detail.

The lid top is a paint skin — the same transfer technique as the vases, but poured specifically for this surface. Bronze, black, silver, and grey with dense cellular development and crater-like ring formations. It reads like a meteorite cross-section or the surface of the moon. Over the skin, a layer of clear resin — poured on top to seal, harden, and protect. The resin makes the surface wipeable and sturdy enough to hold a drink or a book without damage.

The band around the lid edge is black felt with upholstery tacks painted silver — a direct reference to the studded collar Pax used to wear. The tacks are evenly spaced. The detail is small and specific enough that if you know Pax, you see it immediately.

The inside

Motion-activated light.

The interior has a magnetic USB-rechargeable light mounted inside the lid. It’s motion-activated — open the box, the interior illuminates. This is practical. Cat toy storage in a dark box is harder to navigate than it sounds, especially when you’re trying to find a specific wand toy at the request of a cat who is standing on your feet.

The light is charged through the USB port without removing it. It has held a charge reliably.

In use

Working as intended.

The box sits beside the couch as a side table. It holds a drink, a book, a remote. It also holds everything Pax owns that lives in the living room. He sits on top of it regularly — it is the correct height, it is sturdy, and it smells exactly like what he wants.

The latches remain closed. He remains annoyed. The system is working as intended.
